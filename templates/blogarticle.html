Hasan Raheem

Last year, I came across the multi-hyphenate, Hasan Raheem – the artist through a small concert that recently went viral over the internet for being organised during Covid times. That video was as random as his entry into the music scene - there's nothing, absolutely nothing planned about his arrival, but one look at his numbers and the number of tracks he throws every now then is enough to know that he is here to stay.

Well, clearly it wasn't the best time to gather a crowd, however (secretly I'm glad he did) because now I'm hooked to each and every song of Hasan Raheem.Hasan still getting accustomed to screaming fans and over-pouring affection.

Was this the first time I heard Hasan Raheem drown me in an abyss created by his effortlessly strained, but vibing voice? No, I realised I was already a fan of a snippet of “Aisay Kaisay” that I had once heard on Instagram, and look at me now, I'm writing this to why you should be going gaga over this talented boy from Gilgit-Baltistan, who by the way is a very cool doctor in the making!(Dr. He Is!!!)

His take on soul, rnb and indie music in a pool that's brimming with pop and pop rock music Pakistani artists, Hasan Raheem is like a breath of fresh air (and kinda somewhere in the zone of the likes of Shamoon Ismail, no?) I love them both.

Aisay Kaisay is the very first song that introduced me to Hasan Raheem and of the major reasons why it's still on repeat. (8 Aisay Kaisay repeats on my clg route)"Oh, Aisay Kaisay is that one track that is closest to me," he comments. "It was shot in one take - the first take. I just really like the whole feel to it.”I too like it alot.From the subtle shifts to the sounds and the way he plays around in that pink hoodie kinda ropes me in:)

You may hate a thing that is good for you

Having that void in your heart can be painful. The one that doesn’t quite feel right—like something is missing, but you just can’t seem to put your finger on what; it is borderline painful. I felt it too. Days pass by, you don’t like to see or be around family or friends, you can’t focus at work, you don’t quite feel like eating anything. Depression? Anxiety? No, no; it is a disease and needs to be treated, but not a tangible disease, no—it is a spiritual one.

I was convinced by the ayah that says: “You may dislike something although it is good for you, or like something, although it is bad for you” – Qur'an (2:216)

The only thing that was steady during certain awful time was my prayers; even after all those feelings of loss, I kept up with my prayers. I didn't let that go because deep down I knew the tough days would pass with the help of Allah (SWT).But the time awaited.I knew He would guide me—He is Al Hadiy and Ar Rashid. I knew He loved people who were committed to Him. “God loves those who are steadfast” – Quran (3:146). Deep down, I knew Allah (SWT) tests those whom He loves, and clearly He was testing me because He loves me. The thing I hated, that pain that sorrow and darkness, led me to be closer to him; those dark days brought me the greatest gift.

It’s 2014, Crocs Are Cool Again!

Crocs are cool? How the hell did that happen? This past year, in the midst of the pandemic and an economic downturn that hurt nearly every industry, the brand everyone loves to hate saw their strongest revenue in history.

After their early-aughts heyday, Crocs suffered. In 2008, with “mountains of shoes and no one to buy them,” Crocs suffered a loss of $185 million (Washington Post). The novelty of the rubber clogs wore off, and Crocs slipped from our radar. Until recently.

The Crocs Effect is being felt even outside the company's comfy foam walls. Over the past couple of years, labels like Gucci experimented with a Crocs look alike. The new Yeezy Foam Runner, with its foam construction and Swiss-cheese design, appears to be heavily indebted to the Croc. Imagine sticking a Jibbitz shaped like one of Kanye’s SHERP ATVs into one of those perforations. Perfection!

I think Crocs is really the platonic ideal of a pandemic shoe: they slip on and off easily and, for despite the misgivings anyone might have about how they look, are undeniably comfortable as hell.
